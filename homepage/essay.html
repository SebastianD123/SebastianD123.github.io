The Essay

Here's my "Write Your New York" essay. I based my essay on a local deli that's been part of my life for as long as I can remember.

Steve's Deli

It’s not some crazy idea to say that the city happens to be a relentless whirlwind of ambition and change—because it is, and it can be tough to manage when everything hits you all at once. It’s a place where dreams are born and tested every day, where every borough boasts its own flavor, and where the unwavering pace of things can leave you gasping for breath. For me, though, amidst the chaos of the Bronx, there’s a place that defies the law of the city’s rush and has become a place I can call home.

Steve’s Deli remains an anchor in the city where trends come and go in the blink of an eye, a place where the past is celebrated just as much as the present. To me, the deli is like a time capsule, a reminder that the city has a history that stretches back long before I was born, filled with the stories of its residents. The deli, as a living chapter in my life, is a living chapter in many others’ lives, both older and younger.

The deli is a far cry from the upscale establishments you’d find somewhere in Manhattan, bearing the weathered marks of years gone by. It doesn’t boast the fragrant smell of exotic cuisine or the sophisticated look of such eateries. There also aren't servers waiting at every corner to cater to your every whim. Instead, it carries an unmistakable scent of nostalgia, of community, and of the shared stories that have transformed the place into a web of interconnected lives through time.

The linoleum floors have absorbed the imprints of countless footsteps, whispered secrets, and shared laughter over the decades. The vinyl-covered booths, although showing fair signs of wear and tear, have nurtured countless friendships and conversations, their echoes resonating with the countless generations who’ve found solace in the place since its opening in the 1990s. This kind of wear over time goes to show how the deli isn’t a place that only I’ve ever called home. Many people before me have considered this place home, and many people after me will continue this tradition till the day the deli is no longer. So, even though it may not be a conventional image of the city, the deli encapsulates a deeper essence—an essence that goes beyond location, one that turns it into a sanctuary amidst the ever-changing city.

The deli chooses to excel in simplicity over all else. It’s not about reinventing classic dishes; it’s about perfecting them. If it’s not on their small menu, they won't be afraid to let you know that they just can’t make it. But the menu, while small, is a testament to quality over quantity, as the food they do serve is the kind of food that makes you close your eyes and really savor each bite. The sandwiches are piled high with carefully chosen meats, crisp lettuce, and juicy tomatoes, all nestled between bread baked in-house. Each bite is a symphony of flavors that takes you back to a time when food was made with passion, not just as a commodity.

Of course, the deli isn’t just a spot for a quick meal, or a spot to just get a bag of chips and leave. It’s a gathering place for those in the know in the East Bronx community. Here, it’s where friends will meet for a chat and have an annoyingly long stay, where families will come together for a hearty meal, and where new connections are formed on a daily. It isn’t just about the food or coffee; it’s about the people who walk through its doors.

If you ask me, Steve’s Deli, with its weathered charm, commitment to simplicity, and unwavering authenticity, stands as a testament to the enduring spirit of the Bronx and the city itself. It’s a place where past meets present, where the heartbeat of a neighborhood can be heard in the clinking of glasses and the laughter of friends. This unassuming corner of the Bronx has carved out its own space in the grand tapestry of the city, a place where stories are shared and memories are made. Above all, though, it’s a place I can call home.
